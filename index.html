Been thinking a lot about a chat I had awhile back with my former colleague about unexpected language. What interests me is the idea of unexpected language with clear intention, where semantic violations create meaning through contradiction. When Shakespeare writes "two distincts, division none" or "saw division grow together," he breaks semantic rules precisely to capture something beyond the reach of ordinary language.
